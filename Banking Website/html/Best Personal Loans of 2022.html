Personal loans let you borrow money to meet a variety of financial goals. Whether youd like to consolidate high-interest credit debt, cover an unexpected car repair, or pay for a home improvement project, a personal loan can help. 

With the number of personal loan lenders available, it can be difficult to choose one. But the best lenders are known for competitive rates, flexible terms, and transparent fees. Plus they offer loans to borrowers with various credit situations so you can find one that is right for you.

Why We Chose It

With a SoFi personal loan, you wont have to worry about any fees, like origination fees or prepayment fees. Youll get unemployment protection, which will allow you to temporarily change your payments and even get some help finding a new job, and other benefits. SoFi loans are also flexible, meaning you can use them on credit card consolidation, home improvements, family planning, weddings, and anything in between.

Other perks of a SoFi loan include same-day funding in some cases, a quick application process that allows you to pre-qualify with no impact on your credit score, and high loan limits for qualified borrowers. Repayment terms are also flexible, with some loans having up to 84 months. 

You do need a 650 credit score to qualify for a SoFi loan, and loans are available in all states except Mississippi. Loans are unsecured, which means you wont need to put down any collateral, but remember that SoFi is exclusively an online lender, so there are no physical branches.

Discover also offers a mobile app you may download to manage your payments and free credit score monitoring.

Guide for Choosing the Best Personal Loans 

Should You Consider a Personal Loan?

If you dont have the cash on hand to cover an expense, you might want to consider getting a personal loan. It can be an effective way to pay for things like consolidating high-interest debt, an emergency expense, home improvements, or even something like a boat or vacation. 

Before you take one out, though, consider if there are other ways you might be able to pay for something and the potential implications to your credit score and your budget (since you will need to repay the loan). 

Youll also want to carefully consider from which lender you get a personal loan. Theyre typically offered by banks, credit unions, and online lenders. If you already have an account at a certain bank or credit union, you might want to explore their loan options, and you might be able to get better terms (even if it takes a little longer to get the funds). But if you prefer a fast application and approval process, an online lender is likely your best bet. 

How to Compare Personal Loan Lenders

When you shop around for a personal loan, its a good idea to compare these factors:

  • APR: Annual percentage rate or APR is the total cost youll pay each year to borrow money, expressed as a percentage. The lower your APR, the less youll spend on your personal loan. 
  • Loan Amounts: Loan amounts vary greatly by lender. The purpose of your loan will help you determine whether youre searching for a small amount around $250 or a larger amount of up to $100,000.
  • Terms: Terms refer to how long you have to repay your loan. While a longer term may lower your monthly payments, it will increase your total interest costs. 
  • Funding Time: Fortunately, most lenders offer fast funding. This may be the same day you apply, within 24 hours, or in a few business days. If you have emergency expenses, fast funding should be a priority. 
  • Customer Service: Theres a good chance youll need support with your loan at some point in time. Look for lenders with high customer service ratings who can help you via phone, email, and online chat. 

How to Apply for a Personal Loan

Once youve made a decision, its time to apply for a personal loan. Most lenders will let you go through the process online. Typically, youll need to fill out a short form and provide the following information:

  • Full name
  • Address
  • Phone number
  • Email
  • Date of birth
  • Social Security number
  • Desired loan amount
  • Intended loan use
  • Employment status
  • Monthly income
  • Monthly housing cost

You might also be asked to submit certain documents to verify your identity, address, and income. These include a utility bill, government-issued ID, like a drivers license or passport, and pay stubs or tax forms. Depending on the lender, you might also have a credit check and if you have a joint applicant, they will likely be asked to provide the same documentation. 

A decision about your application can be made the same day or it could take up to two (or more weeks, again depending on the lender). Once a decision is made, you should receive funds relatively quickly. 

Frequently Asked Questions

What is a Personal Loan?

Offered by banks, credit unions, and online lenders, a personal loan is a form of credit you can use to pay for a variety of expenses. Usually, its unsecured and doesnt require collateral or something valuable you own. While personal loans vary greatly, many of them offer easy applications, flexible terms, and fast funding. 

How Does a Personal Loan Impact Your Credit? 

A personal loan can help or hurt your credit. As long as you make your payments on time, every time, your credit score will increase. A personal loan may also improve your credit mix and lower your credit utilization ratio, which can also benefit your credit. On the flip side, late payments may hurt your credit. 

What Can I Use a Personal Loan For?

In most cases, you can use a personal loan to cover any expense. The top reasons for personal loans include debt consolidation, home remodeling, moving, emergency expenses, and wedding costs. Keep in mind that some lenders impose restrictions and wont let you use a personal loan on things like college or gambling. Make sure you verify with the lender before applying for the loan that you can use the funds for your intended purpose.
